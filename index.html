Engineering Methodology

Built on structured
engineering logic

Every number in a Nova Metrics design is derived from structured calculations — not rules of thumb. The platform meets NRS 097-2-1 and NERSA SSEG standards because it was built that way, not because it was added as a compliance layer afterwards.

01
Load-based solar sizing
Solar is sized to cover daytime consumption plus overnight battery recharge, using industry-standard performance ratios and system availability benchmarks. Matched to the most suitable panel configuration from your live catalogue — not a generic estimate.
Solar Array
02
Multi-gate battery validation
Three checks before accepting any battery configuration: capacity headroom above required storage, depth-of-discharge within design targets, and guaranteed autonomy runtime. Configured to protect LiFePO4 cycle life with real per-battery DoD and efficiency values.
Battery
03
Inverter sizing & compliance check
Inverter sized with an engineering safety margin over peak load. DC/AC ratio validated in alignment with NRS 097-2-1 parameters. Every inverter in your catalogue is cross-referenced against the South African Master Inverter Compliance Register — 2,440+ certified models on record.
Inverter · NRS 097-2-1
04
365-day net yield modelling
Annual production estimates apply performance ratios, system availability factors, and panel degradation assumptions aligned with South African norms. Location-specific irradiance pulled from 152 SA locations. Specific yield benchmarked against national reference values.
Yield
05
Engineering-grade financials
25-year indicative projections using NERSA-aligned tariff escalation, NPV and IRR modelling with industry-standard discount rates, maintenance provisions, insurance estimates, and battery replacement lifecycle costs. All figures clearly marked as indicative estimates.
NPV · IRR · Financials
06
Engagement pipeline intelligence
Once a proposal is sent, the platform tracks email opens, proposal views, PDF downloads, and read time. A follow-up priority signal — Hot, Warm, Cold — tells you exactly who to call and when, without manually tracking every lead in a spreadsheet.

Installation Pipeline

Five steps, every job,
every time — no exceptions

Every won job moves through a defined pipeline. Each stage unlocks when the previous one is complete, creating a clean audit trail from site assessment to customer sign-off.

1
Site Visit Assessment
Roof condition, electrical infrastructure, shading analysis, cable run, meter type, DB board, and recommended system. If specs differ from the proposal, a design change is flagged — triggering the client approval workflow.
2
Installation Preparation
Team lead, team members, scheduled date, and full equipment spec — panel brand and watts, inverter, battery. Locks in exactly what goes to site.
3
Installation Checklist
Pre-installation, structural and mounting, electrical wiring, and commissioning checklists. Tick off with notes as the team works through each item.
4
Final Sign-off
As-installed system specs, any outstanding punch list items, inspector name and date. Marks the installation complete before handover proceeds.
5
Customer Handover
COC number, issuing electrician, grid approval status, customer training confirmation, warranty details, and a printable Handover Certificate signed by both parties.
Design Change Management

When the site doesn’t match
the original proposal

It happens. The assessor gets on-site and the roof orientation is wrong, the DB board needs upgrading, or the cable run is twice what was assumed. The original proposal specs no longer fit — and the client needs to know.

Nova Metrics handles this properly. When a site visit flags a design change, the platform generates a revised proposal version, calculates the exact delta against the original accepted specs, notifies the client with a secure approval link, and holds the job until a decision is made. Nothing moves forward on an unapproved change.

  • ✓ Installer flags the change with a reason and category at site visit
  • ✓ Original proposal specs stored and compared to revised design
  • ✓ Delta calculated: system kW, battery kWh, cost, and percentage change
  • ✓ New proposal version created with full revision history
  • ✓ Client notified with a unique secure approval link — they accept or decline
  • ✓ Job locked until client decision is recorded — full audit trail throughout
Why this matters

Most solar businesses handle design changes over WhatsApp, with no record of what the client agreed to or when. Nova Metrics turns an awkward conversation into a documented, timestamped approval process — protecting both the installer and the client.
