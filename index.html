Decision Engineering

Designed to produce the
right answer every time

Every number in a Nova Metrics proposal is derived from structured engineering models — not rules of thumb. Defensible, repeatable, and aligned with South African standards by design.

01
Load-based solar sizing
Solar is sized to cover daytime consumption plus overnight battery recharge — not a rough multiple of peak load. Calculated using industry-standard performance ratios and system availability benchmarks, then matched to the most suitable panel configuration from your live catalogue.
Solar Array
02
LiFePO4 battery design validation
Multi-gate engineering checks before accepting any battery configuration: capacity headroom, depth-of-discharge within design targets, and guaranteed autonomy runtime. Optimised to maximise LiFePO4 cycle life with industry-standard round-trip efficiency applied.
Battery
03
NRS 097-2-1 alignment check
Inverter sized with engineering safety margin applied to peak load. DC/AC ratio validated in alignment with NRS 097-2-1 parameters. Every uploaded inverter is automatically cross-referenced against the South African Master Inverter Compliance Register (2,440+ certified models).
Inverter · NRS 097-2-1
04
365-day net yield modelling
Annual production estimates apply performance ratios, system availability factors, and panel degradation assumptions aligned with South African industry norms. Location-specific irradiance from a 150+ location South African database. Specific yield benchmarked against national references.
Yield
05
Engineering-grade financial projections
25-year indicative projections using NERSA-aligned tariff escalation, industry-standard discount rates for NPV, IRR modelling, maintenance provisions, insurance estimates, and battery replacement lifecycle costs. All outputs are indicative estimates subject to site conditions and tariff changes.
NPV · IRR · Financials
06
Engagement pipeline intelligence
After a proposal is sent, the platform tracks email opens, proposal views, PDF downloads, and read time — subject to applicable privacy policies. Follow-up priority signal (Hot, Warm, Cold) so installers know exactly who to contact and when.

Installation Pipeline

Five structured steps
from site visit to handover

Every won job moves through a defined pipeline. Each step unlocks when the previous one is complete, creating an automatic audit trail from site assessment to customer sign-off.

1
Site Visit Assessment
Roof condition, electrical infrastructure, shading analysis, cable run, meter type, DB board, and recommended system. All captured on-site, saved instantly.
2
Installation Preparation
Team lead, team members, installation date, and full equipment spec — panel brand, inverter size, battery brand and capacity. Locks in what goes to site.
3
Installation Checklist
Structured checklists for pre-installation, structural and mounting, electrical wiring, and full commissioning. Tick-off with notes as the team works.
4
Final Sign-off
As-installed system specs, punch list of outstanding items, inspector name, and date inspected. Marks the installation as complete before handover.
5
Customer Handover
COC number, issuing electrician, grid approval status, customer training confirmation, and a printable Handover Certificate — signed by both parties.
