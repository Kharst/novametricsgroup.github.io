Decision Engineering

Designed to get the
right answer every time

Every number in a Nova Metrics proposal is derived from structured models. No rules of thumb. No spreadsheet estimates. Defensible outputs, by default.

01
Load-based solar sizing
Solar is sized to cover daytime consumption plus overnight battery recharge — not a rough multiple of peak load. Required energy calculated correctly, then matched to the smallest adequate panel configuration from your live catalogue.
Solar Array
02
Stress-validated battery selection
The battery engine iterates every model in your catalogue to find the cheapest combination that simultaneously achieves: ≥110% capacity headroom, ≤55% nightly stress, and guaranteed runtime — using average overnight draw, not peak load.
Battery
03
SANS 10142-1 inverter matching
Inverter is sized to handle peak AC load with the NRS 097-2-3 30% safety margin. DC/AC ratio is validated against SANS limits: amber warning at 1.30, red flag above 1.33 — with status shown in the UI.
Inverter
04
365-day yield modelling
Annual production uses 365 days with real panel derating (temperature, soiling, wiring losses). Location-specific GTI drawn from your database — and if a location isn't in the DB, the installer can still proceed with a sensible default.
Yield
05
Compounding financial projections
Simple payback is shown, but real payback is calculated by simulating 25 years of SA tariff escalation — NERSA-aligned at 12.74% for years 1–3, then 5.4% thereafter. The net gain figure matches exactly what appears in the PDF.
Financials
06
Engagement pipeline intelligence
After a proposal is sent, the platform tracks email opens, proposal views, PDF downloads, and time-on-page. Installers see a follow-up priority: Hot, Warm, Cold — so they know exactly who to call and when.
